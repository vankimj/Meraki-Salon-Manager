# Founders' Cohort Recruitment Scripts

**Purpose:** Six channel-specific scripts for getting your first 25 Founders' Members. Same core message, different framing for each context.

**Universal principles:**
1. **Lead with value, not the ask.** Why should they care goes BEFORE what you want.
2. **Specific over general.** Reference one thing about their salon, not a template.
3. **Peer, not vendor.** You own a salon. They own a salon. Skip the sales voice.
4. **Easy yes, easy no.** Make the action one click. Make the rejection painless.

---

## 1. Cold email — to a salon owner you've researched

**When to use:** A salon owner you've identified through Google Maps, Yelp, or social media. Subject line is what gets opened.

**Subject line options (test these):**
- "Owner-to-owner question, [Salon Name]"
- "Quick question for a fellow [city] salon owner"
- "Saw [specific thing about their salon]"

**Email body:**

```
Hi [first name],

I own Meraki Nail Studio in Columbus — about 10 techs. Saw [specific thing
about their salon — e.g. "your post about the vintage gel work last week" or
"your 4.9-star Google rating from 200+ reviews"]. Impressive.

Reaching out because I built a software platform for my own salon (after years
of cobbling together GG + Mailchimp + a dozen other tools), and I'm letting a
small group of other salon owners use it free for life as part of a Founders'
Cohort. 25 spots, in exchange for honest feedback over the first 6 months.

Three reasons I thought of you:
1. You're [size — e.g. "5-tech, similar scale to mine"]
2. [Something specific from their public presence — e.g. "your text-heavy comms
   style suggests you'd care about a unified inbox"]
3. [Something else — geography, segment, recent activity]

If you're at all curious, the site's at plumenexus.com (still in soft-launch,
won't show up on Google yet, you'll need the link). The Founders' Cohort
application form is at [link].

Or just reply with a Calendly window and we'll grab 20 minutes — no pitch, just
peer-to-peer.

— Jonathan VanKim
Owner, Meraki Nail Studio
hello@plumenexus.com
```

**Volume:** Send 5-10 per day, max. More than that = generic blast = low response rate.
**Expect:** 15-25% open rate, 2-5% reply rate (cold). Higher if your subject line is specific.

## 3. Walk-in script — Columbus salons

**When to use:** You're physically going to local salons. Best time: Tuesday/Wednesday between 11am and 2pm (slow window for most salons).

**At the front desk (60 seconds):**

> "Hi — is the owner around? My name's Jonathan, I own Meraki Nail Studio over on [neighborhood]. I'm not selling anything, I'm working on a salon software project and looking for owners to give me feedback. Just wanted to see if I could leave my card and a quick one-pager."

**If owner is there (and willing to talk briefly):**

> "Thanks for a minute. Quick context — I run a 10-tech nail salon. Got frustrated with [GG/Square/etc.] enough that I built my own software for it. Now I'm in the testing phase with other owners. Looking for 25 salons to be Founders' Members — free for life, in exchange for honest feedback over 6 months. Would you be open to a 20-minute coffee chat sometime to give me your take? Not selling — just learning what other owners need."

**Hand them:** the one-pager + your card. Get their email if you can. Walk out within 5 minutes — don't overstay.

**If they're busy:** "Totally understand — here's my card and a one-pager. If it's interesting, my email's on it." Walk out smiling.

**Your conversion rate target:** 1 in 5 will say yes to the coffee. So 10 walk-ins = 2 conversations = 1 likely Founders' Member. That's a great hit rate.

## 4. Facebook group post — owners' communities

**When to use:** After lurking in a salon-owner FB group for 2+ weeks. Don't post in your first week — read first, post second.

**Post format:**

```
Owner here, sharing something I'm working on — looking for 25 salon owners to
be Founders' Members of a new platform.

Quick context: I own Meraki Nail Studio in Columbus (10 techs). Got tired of
running the salon on a duct-taped mix of GlossGenius + Mailchimp + a paper
tip jar. Started building what I actually wanted as a software project.
After a year of using it at Meraki, I'm letting other owners join in.

The deal:
- Free Solo plan for life if you sign up by June 30, 2027
- In exchange: honest feedback, ~30 min/month for the first 6 months
- 25 spots in this cohort

Looking for owners with 1-15 staff. Any segment — nail, hair, spa, brows,
barbershop. Mostly USA but open.

If interested, the application's at [link]. I read every one personally.

Happy to answer questions in the comments — no DMs needed.
```

**Important:** Follow the group's promotion rules. Many require "no self-promo" or "self-promo in pinned thread only." Read the rules. If posting is allowed, you're fine. If it's not, DO NOT spam — find another channel.

## 5. LinkedIn post — your personal network

**When to use:** Once a week, max. Use your personal profile.

```
Two years ago, I bought a nail salon. I'm a software engineer by training.

Within six months, I'd patched together six different tools to run the salon
and was still missing appointments, losing tip data, and forgetting to follow
up with clients. So I started building what I actually needed.

Today, that thing is Plume Nexus — a salon management platform that runs my
10-tech salon end-to-end and is now ready for other owners.

Opening a Founders' Cohort: 25 salons, free Solo plan for life, in exchange
for honest feedback over 6 months.

If you own a salon (or know someone who does), apply at plumenexus.com.
I read every application personally.

Not posting often about this — just wanted to share where I've spent my
weekends for the last 18 months.
```

**Engagement strategy:** Reply to every comment. Connect with anyone who comments. Don't pitch in DMs unless they DM you first.

## 6. Reddit post — r/salonowners or similar

**When to use:** After you've contributed comments to the subreddit for 3-4 weeks. Cold posts get downvoted; established accounts don't.

**Title:** "Salon owner here, built software for my own salon, looking for beta users"

```
Hey all — long-time lurker, first time posting about this.

I own Meraki Nail Studio in Columbus, OH (10 nail techs). I'm also a software
engineer. Spent the last 18 months building a salon management platform
because nothing on the market fit how I actually run the business.

It's now in production at my salon and ready for others. I'm calling it
Plume Nexus.

Looking for 25 salon owners to be Founders' Members. The deal: free Solo
plan for life, in exchange for ~30 min/month of feedback over 6 months.

What it does: scheduling, POS, two-way SMS + email inbox, marketing, AI
reporting, voice command booking. Replaces a half-dozen tools.

What it's NOT: another GlossGenius clone. The AI features alone are stuff
nobody else ships.

Site: plumenexus.com (in soft launch, won't show on Google yet, link required).

Happy to answer any questions in the comments. AMA-style if folks are
curious about anything specific.

— Jonathan
```

**Important:** This is a high-trust audience. Don't fake it. Don't shill in comments. Be real.

## Tracking sheet (recommended)

For each contact attempt, log:

| Field | Example |
|---|---|
| Date | 2026-05-15 |
| Channel | Cold email · Warm intro · Walk-in · FB group · LinkedIn · Reddit · Other |
| Salon name | Sarah's Beauty Bar |
| Owner name | Sarah Smith |
| Email / phone | sarah@sarahsbb.com |
| First touch | Email sent / coffee scheduled / DMed |
| Replied? | Y/N + when |
| Outcome | Demo booked · Founders' Cohort applied · Not interested · Ghost |
| Notes | Wanted to see voice command demo specifically |

After 100 outreach attempts you'll know:
- Which channel converts best for your niche
- What subject lines / hooks work
- What time of week / month is best for outreach
- Where to spend more time vs less

That data should drive your phase-2 outreach strategy.

---

## What "success" looks like in 8 weeks

- 100+ outreach attempts across 4-6 channels
- 30+ replies / conversations
- 20-25 Founders' Cohort applications
- 15-20 accepted into the cohort
- 10+ activated salons (logged in within 2 weeks of acceptance)
- 5+ verbatim testimonials you can use later

If you hit those numbers in 8 weeks, you're well ahead of typical SaaS launch curves. If you don't, look at the funnel to find where the drop-off is and adjust.
