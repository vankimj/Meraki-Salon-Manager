# Personal Network Worksheet — Finding Salon Owners You Already Know

**Purpose:** Your network is bigger than you think. This worksheet helps you find the salon owners — and people one step away from salon owners — who are already in your phone, calendar, and client book. Use it once, then again every 90 days.

**Why this beats cold outreach 10:1:**
- Warm intros convert at ~30% vs cold at ~3-5%
- People who already know you skip the "is this a scam?" filter
- Even non-ICP contacts can be referral bridges
- Costs $0 — only your time

**Time to complete:** 60-90 minutes the first time. Block it on your calendar.

---

## The five circles of warm prospects

You'll find people in five categories. Each gets a different approach.

### Circle 1 — Direct ICP (other salon owners you already know)
Other people who own or operate salons, spas, studios. The fastest path to your first customers.

### Circle 2 — Adjacent ICP (service providers in your industry)
Massage therapists, hair stylists, estheticians, brow techs at OTHER establishments. Some are owners; many are renters/contractors with high decision influence on the owner.

### Circle 3 — Network connectors (people who know LOTS of salon owners)
Beauty supply reps, sales reps, distributors, cosmetology school instructors, beauty industry consultants, accountants/lawyers who specialize in salons. One conversation here = 10-50 introductions.

### Circle 4 — Your own clients who own businesses
Your Meraki client list contains people who own restaurants, dental practices, real estate offices — anyone with a small-business owner mindset. They're not your ICP, but they know other small-business owners.

### Circle 5 — Personal life (friends, family, neighbors)
Your hairdresser since you were 10. Your aunt who does nails out of her house. Your college roommate who bought a yoga studio. The "who in my life works in beauty/wellness?" inventory.

## Prioritization — who to approach first

After brainstorming, you'll have 30-100 names. Don't approach all of them. Score each on:

| Criterion | 1 (low) | 3 (med) | 5 (high) |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Quality of relationship** | Met once | Acquaintance | Close, comfortable |
| **Path-to-ICP** | No connection | One step away | Direct ICP |
| **Likely decision-maker** | Just an employee | Influencer | Owner / GM |
| **Approachability** | Awkward | Neutral | Natural fit |
| **Potential reach** | One person | Few connections | Network of dozens |

**Score 18-25 → approach in week 1**
**Score 13-17 → approach in week 2-3**
**Score 8-12 → approach in week 4 or skip**
**Score under 8 → skip for this round**

## Channel-specific scripts

How you reach out depends on the relationship and the circle.

### Circle 1 — Salon owner you know (high-trust)

**Channel:** Text or DM.

> "Hey [name], hope you're doing well! Question — I built some salon software for Meraki and I'm opening up a small group of other salon owners as 'Founders' Members.' Free for life. Wondering if you'd want to take a look? No pressure either way, just thought of you because [specific reason]."

### Circle 1 — Salon owner you know (acquaintance)

**Channel:** Email or LinkedIn message.

> "Hi [name] — long time no talk. I've spent the last year building a salon management platform for Meraki, and I'm now letting other owners in. I'm looking for 25 'Founders' Members' to use it free for life in exchange for honest feedback. Thought of you because [specific reason]. Worth a 20-min call?"

### Circle 2 — Your own service provider (haircut, massage, etc.)

**Channel:** In person at end of appointment, or text after.

(See [INTERVIEW-SCRIPT.md](INTERVIEW-SCRIPT.md) and the standalone scripts in this doc — but treat them with extra care to protect the working relationship.)

### Circle 3 — Network connector

**Channel:** Email or in-person at next visit.

> "Hi [name] — I'm working on something I'd love your perspective on. I built a software platform for salons (used at Meraki today) and I'm looking for the right early customers. You probably know dozens of salon owners through your work — would love to hear which ones you think are particularly forward-thinking, especially anyone tired of [GG / Square / Vagaro / etc.]. Could I buy you coffee or lunch sometime? Pure picking-your-brain, no ask attached."

The play here is to get them talking — they'll naturally start brainstorming who to introduce you to.

### Circle 4 — Your Meraki client who owns a business

**Channel:** In person at end of their appointment, OR a personal text.

> "Hey [name], totally separate from Meraki — I've been building a software platform for salon owners and I know you run [their business]. Different industry, but I'd love to ask you a few questions about what software you wish existed. Coffee sometime? My treat."

This isn't asking them to buy — it's asking them as a fellow small-business owner. Most will say yes.

### Circle 5 — Personal life contact

**Channel:** Whatever you normally use.

> "Hey [name] — random ask. I'm building software for salon owners. You [know my friend / are family / etc.], so I'm not pitching you, but if you happen to know any salon or spa owners I should talk to, I'd love an intro. Trying to find 25 'Founders' Members' to use it free for life and give honest feedback."

## Bridging contacts — the most underused move

Not everyone you reach out to is ICP. Some will be **bridges**.

When someone says "I'm not a salon owner but my [sister/friend/neighbor] is," IMMEDIATELY:

1. Get the connection: "Would you be willing to introduce us?"
2. Make it easy: "I can write a short intro email — you'd just need to forward it." (Save them the labor.)
3. Offer something in return: "If they sign up for the Founders' Cohort, I'll send you a thank-you gift card."

Most warm intros happen because YOU make it easy for the connector. Write the intro draft for them.

**Sample intro email you give the bridge to forward:**

```
Subject: Connecting you — [Salon Owner First Name] → Jonathan @ Plume Nexus

Hi [Salon Owner],

Quick intro — Jonathan VanKim owns Meraki Nail Studio in Columbus and built a
salon software platform he's now letting other owners use. He's looking for
"Founders' Members" — free for life in exchange for feedback. Knowing you,
I thought he'd be worth 15 minutes of your time.

Jonathan, [Salon Owner] runs [Salon Name] in [City]. [One specific thing
about their salon that's interesting.]

I'll let you two take it from here.

[Your bridge contact]
```

Bridge contacts can produce 5-20 intros each. Treat them like gold.

| Bridges they offered | "Sister owns Beautique LA" |
| Notes | "Currently on Vagaro, hates per-staff pricing" |

After you've worked through 50 contacts, look at the data:
- Which circle converted best? (Spend more time there next round.)
- Which channel had the highest reply rate?
- How many bridges did each contact offer? (The connectors are your best repeat resource.)

## Refresh cadence

Run this whole worksheet once now, then every 90 days. Your network grows as Meraki grows. New clients come in, new vendor reps cycle through, new industry events fill your calendar with new contacts.

The list you build today will be 30% bigger in 6 months — IF you remember to look.
